SYSTEM TERMINAL
CHRONICLE ENTRY — THE QUIET OBSERVER
There was a time when silence felt safer than speaking. Not because words didn’t exist, but because every sentence felt heavy like stepping onto thin glass and waiting for it to crack beneath your weight. I used to sit at the edge of rooms, listening more than living, watching conversations unfold like scenes from a story that never asked me to be part of it. People laughed easily. They moved without hesitation. And I wondered how they carried themselves without the constant noise inside their heads. Nights were quieter, but not peaceful. The mind replayed old moments, unfinished thoughts, and questions without answers. Sometimes I wrote things down just to prove I was still here, still thinking, still feeling. A terminal screen became a strange kind of refuge a place where words stayed exactly as they were typed, untouched by misunderstanding. I learned that being quiet does not mean being empty. It means holding storms behind steady eyes. It means choosing when to speak instead of shouting into the void. And even when everything feels uncertain, there is still a small light that refuses to disappear not loud, not dramatic, just persistent. Maybe this page is nothing more than a pause between chapters. Maybe it is proof that stories don’t always need movement to exist. Some moments are meant to stay still, preserved like a memory inside an old machine that continues to glow long after everyone else has gone.
